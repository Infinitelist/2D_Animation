What is 2D animation?

Well! It is the art of movement within a two-dimensional space; characters are shown frame by frame in a linear progression that leads to a suspenseful and fulfilling storyline.

  • 1834 - William George Horner invented the Zoetrope to capture the illusion of motion.

  • 1877 - Charles-Émile Reynaud invented the Praxinoscope an improved version of the Zoetrope.

  • 1906 - Émile Cohl created the first-ever short film animation by hand called Fantasmagorie.

  • 1928 - Walt Disney's release, Steamboat Willie, featuring the iconic character Mickey Mouse, kick-started the golden age of animation.

  • 1937 - The first full-length animated feature film, Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs, is released by Walt Disney.

  • 1960's - Introduce us to the first-ever full-length Television animated series called The Flintstones.

  • 1994 - The Lion King showcased the blending of traditional hand-drawn animation with digital technology.

  • 2009 - Disney Studios returned to a hand-drawn style with the feature film The Princess and the Frog.
